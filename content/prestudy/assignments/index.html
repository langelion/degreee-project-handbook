    Assignment A2¶

    Task¶

    Assignment A2 concerns the pre-study.

    The process is divided into four stages (A2.0 - A2.4).

    Prerequisite¶

    Approved project specification

    A2.0 - Explore old theses

    Step 1) Search and download three different student theses from the KTH DiVA portal.

    Search category: Independent thesis Advanced level (degree of Master (Two Years))
    Search topic: something relevant for your own project

    Step 2) For each thesis, identify the section(s) derived from the literature study. Look at structure and what topics are discussed.

    Step 3) Add below data to the shared spreadsheet (link provided in separate email)

    • Title
    • How many are the total pages?
    • How many pages are dedicated to content related to the pre-study?
    • How many references are listed in the bibliography (# of references)?

    A2.1 - Pre-study step 1

    Perform the literature search to collect candidate papers relevant for the pre-study. Organize your literature and take notes on the papers while reading.

    When a significant amount of literature has been found, then use the notes to roughly plan your pre-study according to the instructions.

    • Which topics (sections) will be discussed around in the pre-study, and with what granularity?
    • Which are the main sources that will be used for each section?
    • What are the main arguments that justifies the research methodology that will be used to answer the research question?

    Report above to the academic supervisor in a simple and informal format (e.g. sections with bullet points). Time dedication: about two weeks.

    A2.2 - Pre-study step 2

    Write the pre-study](./writing.md). Time dedication: about one week.

    A2.3 - Peer-reviewing

    Each student will perform one peer-review activity associated to the pre-study. The text to review will be facilitated by the academic supervisor.

    Evaluation¶

    The pre-study is presented in a written report. The report is structured so that it can be recycled as a separate chapter in the final written report.

    Criteria for the background section of the final report:

    The scientific field of work is well introduced. The background contains a written review of previous research and development, and the student's own work is designed in accordance with the conclusions of this review. Work related to the state of the art of knowledge are described.
